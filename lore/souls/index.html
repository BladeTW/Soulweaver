    Souls

    Souls are the central resource of the mod.

    Almost every piece of progression—from creating Golems to performing rituals—is built around acquiring, understanding, and manipulating souls.

    Every soul is unique, defined by three core attributes:

    • Attunement
    • Zodiac
    • Strength

    Learning how these properties interact is the key to mastering Soulcraft.

    Obtaining Souls

    Souls are obtained from Wandering Spirits found throughout the world.

    When a Wandering Spirit is defeated, it releases an Unstable Soul.

    This soul exists for only a few seconds before collapsing into a violent burst of soul energy.

    To preserve it, the player must quickly capture it using a Soul Jar.

    Failure to do so results in the permanent destruction of the soul.

    Soul Properties

    Every soul possesses three immutable characteristics.

    Attunement

    A soul is naturally aligned with one of two spiritual forces.

    Angel

    Angel souls favour harmony, protection, and stability.

    Creatures carrying Angel souls are generally passive and defensive.

    Angelic souls are commonly used for:

    • Defensive Golems
    • Healing effects
    • Protective rituals
    • Stabilising magical constructs

    Devil

    Devil souls embody conflict, ambition, and raw power.

    Creatures carrying Devil souls are aggressive and far more dangerous.

    Devil souls excel at:

    • Offensive Golems
    • Destructive rituals
    • High-risk magical processes
    • Powerful weapon enchantments

    Neither Attunement is stronger than the other. They simply represent different approaches to Soulcraft.

    Zodiac

    Every soul is attuned to one of the Twelve Celestial Constellations.

    The Zodiac determines how a soul behaves once bound into a construct or used in a ritual.

    The twelve Zodiac signs are:

    Position Zodiac
    1 Aries
    2 Taurus
    3 Gemini
    4 Cancer
    5 Leo
    6 Virgo
    7 Libra
    8 Scorpio
    9 Sagittarius
    10 Capricorn
    11 Aquarius
    12 Pisces

    Each Zodiac grants different behavioural traits and magical affinities.

    For example:

    • Aries favours direct combat.
    • Taurus values endurance.
    • Gemini embraces adaptability.
    • Virgo excels at precision.
    • Scorpio specialises in poison and curses.
    • Pisces prefers agility and evasive tactics.

    The Zodiac also determines compatibility during rituals such as Soul Breeding and multi-soul Golem creation.

    Strength

    Every soul possesses a Strength between 1 and 5.

    Strength represents the amount of spiritual energy contained within the soul.

    Strength Rarity Description
    1 Common Weak but plentiful. Ideal for early-game crafting.
    2 Uncommon Slightly stronger with improved abilities.
    3 Rare Balanced souls suitable for advanced Golems.
    4 Epic Highly sought-after souls with exceptional power.
    5 Legendary Extremely rare souls capable of powering the strongest constructs.

    Strength affects the effectiveness of the soul but does not alter its personality or Zodiac.

    Soul Compatibility

    Not every soul works well with another.

    Compatibility depends on:

    • Attunement
    • Zodiac
    • Certain advanced ritual conditions

    Compatible souls coexist peacefully within a construct.

    Poorly matched souls gradually increase instability, making powerful creations far more difficult to maintain.

    Understanding compatibility is one of the greatest challenges facing an experienced Soulwright.

    Storing Souls

    Captured souls remain safely contained within Soul Jars until they are processed.

    For larger collections, players can build a Soul Repository, a multiblock capable of safely storing and organising hundreds of souls.

    Repositories can sort souls by:

    • Attunement
    • Zodiac
    • Strength

    allowing players to quickly locate the soul required for a ritual or construct.

    Manipulating Souls

    As players progress, they unlock rituals capable of altering and combining souls.

    Examples include:

    • Breeding compatible souls to produce new Zodiac combinations.
    • Purifying corrupted souls.
    • Corrupting purified souls.
    • Binding souls into Golems.
    • Expanding a Golem's soul capacity.
    • Stabilising multi-soul constructs.

    Most advanced rituals require large amounts of Soul Powder and specialised multiblock structures.

    Unstable Souls

    Immediately after a Wandering Spirit is defeated, its soul becomes unstable.

    Unstable Souls:

    • Hover above the ground.
    • Emit bright particles.
    • Grow increasingly volatile over time.
    • Explode if left uncaptured.

    Higher-strength souls release significantly more energy when they destabilise, making them more dangerous to recover.

    Quick reactions and careful preparation are essential when hunting powerful spirits.

    Soul Lifecycle

    A soul passes through several stages during its existence.

    flowchart TD
    A[Living Creature] --> B[Wandering Spirit]
    B --> C[Unstable Soul]

    C -->|Captured| D[Soul Jar]
    D --> E[Rituals / Storage / Golems]

    C -->|Ignored| F[Soul Explosion Lost Forever]

    The Importance of Souls

    Souls are far more than a collectible resource.

    They determine the strength of your Golems, influence the outcome of rituals, unlock advanced crafting recipes, and shape nearly every aspect of progression.

    A successful Soulwright is not simply one who collects the most souls, but one who understands how to harness their unique properties to create powerful and stable constructs.
